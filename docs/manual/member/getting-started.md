# Getting started

Welcome! Soru is where your company manages day-to-day work. It works best on your phone.

## 1. Create your account

You'll usually receive an **invitation link** from your company.

1. Open the invitation link.
2. Create your account (name, email, password) — use the **same email address the invitation was sent to**. Already have an account from another company? Sign in instead; the link takes you to the same place.
3. Set up **two-step verification** — this is required, see below.
4. Accept the invitation when it appears. If you ever lose the link, don't worry: pending invitations also show on your home screen after you sign in.

Invitation links are valid for **14 days** — ask for a new one if yours expired.

## 2. Two-step verification (required)

Your account protects personal data, so a password alone is not enough. At first sign-in you'll set up a **verification app** (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, 1Password…):

1. Open your authenticator app and scan the QR code shown.
2. Enter the 6-digit code to confirm.

From then on, signing in = password + the 6-digit code from your app. If you lose your phone, contact your administrator or support — after confirming your identity they can reset two-step verification. Full details: [Signing in & two-step verification](../concepts/sign-in-and-2fa.md).

## 3. Install the app on your phone

Soru is a web app you can install — no app store needed:

- **Android/Chrome**: menu ⋮ → *Add to Home screen* → *Install*.
- **iPhone/Safari**: Share □↑ → *Add to Home Screen*.

It opens in its own window, with its own icon — like any other app.

## 4. Find your way around (today)

- Your **home screen** lists the companies you belong to and any pending invitations. Tap a company to enter it.
- Inside a company, the **company name at the top left** switches between companies or returns to your list.
- Company screens show the members and (for now) an invitation form. **Business modules — HR, Finance, Production — arrive in upcoming releases**, and this guide grows with them.

## If you work in more than one company

One account works everywhere. Each company sees only its own data about you — your personal profile stays yours.
